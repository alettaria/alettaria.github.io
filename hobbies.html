Traveller and Art Admirer

I am an avid traveller who has been to 18 countries in Asia, Europe, Australia and North America.
My must-do in a trip is to visit local museums and art galleries, spending several days admiring the brilliant art works. I especially appreciate the subtle use of light, shadow and color in paintings, which convey mood and story and evoke endless imagination and sentiments. Dutch Golden Age and Impressionism are my favorates: Rambrandt, Vermeer, Monet, Renoir, Sorolla...I also regard visiting museums and galleries as a way of obtaining new knowledge. Art work is not only about styles or techniques, but also about people and history. Admiring arts is thus a great way to gratify my curiosity about the past time.
The painting used above in the banner is Caspar David Friedrich's Woman at a Window.

Language Learner and Amateur Translator

Before I came to the U.S. and started pursuing career as an analyst, I studied German Language and Literature at Fudan University and University of Tuebingen. In the four years, I dived into poetry and novel, learning from life philosophy of great minds. In my second year, I joined the translation project of a famous German young adult literature series and co-translated one book, in the hope that the series, which discussed openly about teen's concerns - school work, family conflict, friends, love interests...- could evoke self-reflections among Chinese teenagers and guide them through this most sensitive period in life. The book is published in 2018 and is available on Amazon (shameless self-promo!).
I am also quite into Japanese. As a J-pop fan for years, I taught myself Japanese and passed Japanese Language Proficiency Test N1 with top 3% scores in 2017.
